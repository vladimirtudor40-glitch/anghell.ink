02 — Statement

I work in black ink and restraint. A line is a decision you keep for life — so I make few of them, and I make them slowly.

I am drawn to the place where the devotional meets the anatomical — feathers, thorns, ornament, the quiet geometry of the body. My background is in drawing, and I treat skin the way I'd treat fine paper: as something that deserves a held breath before the first mark.

I take on a small number of pieces. Each begins as a conversation and a drawing made for you alone — never a flash repeated. If we work together, you'll leave with something that feels less like a tattoo and more like a small, permanent piece of art.

04 — Access

Request a session.

I open a small number of slots each season. Tell me what you have in mind — the idea, where it lives on the body, roughly how large — and any references that hold the feeling.

Every request is read personally. If your idea and my hand are a match, you'll hear back with next steps and a rough waitlist window. There is no rush here, on either side.
